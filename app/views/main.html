About

The College Scorecard project is intended to provide students and parents a full understanding of the cost and benefits of higher education. This project expands on that intention by providing users the ability to discover and compare schools based on a a school's student profile, costs, and outcomes.

Use the filters below to discover schools that match your prefences. Then you can save and compare schools.

Definitions

All data are from the 2014/15 academic year unless otherwise noted. This is the most recent year of data available.

Field Definition Purpose
Undergraduate Size The total number of undergraduate students enrolled in the current academic year. Small schools are less than 2,000 students, medium schools range from 2,000 to 15,000 students, and large schools range from 15,001 to 60,000 students.
Acceptance Rate The percent of applications accepted by the school. The acceptance rate indicates the liklihood of a student being accepted to a school
SAT average The average combined SAT school for enrolled students
ACT midpoint The ACT median value.
% White The percent of undergraduates who identify as white The percent of white students campus reflects the diversity of the campus.
% First Generation The percent of first generation in college undergraduates.
% Pell Grant Recipients The percent of undergraduates who recieve Pell Grants This field is a proxy to reflect the amount of economic diversity on campus.
% Female The percent of female undergraduates.
% Retained After First Term The percent of first-time, full-time (freshmen) degree-seeking freshmen who start in a fall term and are enrolled in the subsequent term The retention rates reflects the likelihood that a student will persist at the university
Average Net Price of Attendance The average net price students pay for attendance The net price is average cost of attendance after all scholarship and grant monies are applied
Tuition (in State) The in-state tuition cost The cost of class enrollment only
Cost of Attendance Cost of attendance includes tution, fees, room and board. This is the full "sticker price" to attend.
% with Loans The percent of undergraduates who borrowed any federal loans
Debt at Graduation The average principle amount of debt owed by undergraduates after completing a degree program The figure does not include debt accrued by students who transfer or do not complete their degree program
150% Normal Time Complete Rates The percent of first-time, full-time, degree-seeking freshmen who graduate within 150% of normal time Normal time for a 4 year degree institution is 6 years. Data from the 2007 entering freshmen cohort
Post-Graduation Earning 10 Years from Graduation The median income for students 10 years after graduation. Most recent from the 2002 graduating class
% of Graduates Earning More than High School Graduates The percent of students earning more that the average income of high school graduates ($25,000) ten years after graduation
Graduated in 4 Years The percent of first-time, full-time, degree-seeking freshmen who graduate within 100% of normal time Normal time for a 4 year degree institution is 4 years. Data from the 2007 entering freshmen cohort
Graduated in 6 Years See 150% Normal Time Complete Rates
